About FSGAI

Fahr's Syndrome is a rare neurological disorder characterized by abnormal deposits of calcium in areas of the brain that control movement.

Beyond movement issues, Fahr’s Syndrome can also affect cognition and mental health. Many individuals with the condition experience cognitive decline, including memory impairment, difficulty concentrating, and in severe cases, dementia. Psychiatric symptoms such as depression, mood swings, anxiety, and psychosis are also common. These cognitive and psychiatric disturbances can have a profound impact on an individual’s ability to function independently and maintain relationships.

There is currently no cure.

FSGAI's goal is to raise awareness, support research, and help those affected.
